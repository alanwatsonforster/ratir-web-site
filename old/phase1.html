RATIR: Phase 1

The first phase of obtaining time with RATIR is to submit a proposal to the OAN CATT (Comisión de Asignación de Tiempo de Telescopio).

The approximate calendar for normal proposals is:

  • Proposals for the semester January-June
    • Mid-September: call for proposals pubished
    • Mid-October: deadline for proposals
    • Mid-November: results published
  • Proposals for the semester July-December
    • Mid-March: call for proposals pubished
    • Mid-April: deadline for proposals
    • Mid-May: results published

See the CATT web site for more precise dates. Proposals should be submitted in PDF using the CATT LaTeX form. Once a proposal has been awarded time by the CATT, it should proceed to Phase 2.

Outside of the normal call for proposals, the CATT will also consider requests to observe targets of opportunity.

For more information about the proposal process, see the CATT web site or contact Dr. Sergey Zharikov (jao@astro.unam.mx), the Secretary of the CATT.

For information about RATIR, contact ratir@astro.unam.mx.
